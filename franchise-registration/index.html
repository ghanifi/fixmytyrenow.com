Why Mobile Tyre Services Are Growing in the UK

The UK automotive service market is rapidly shifting towards convenience-based solutions. Drivers now expect on-site tyre replacement, emergency roadside assistance, and fast response times — delivered directly to them rather than requiring a garage visit.

  • On-site tyre replacement at home, work, or roadside
  • Emergency assistance without needing to move a damaged vehicle
  • Same-day and next-day availability that garages cannot match
  • Mobile service demand growing year-on-year across all UK cities

Traditional garages are losing demand to mobile operators who deliver faster, more flexible service. This creates a sustained, high-volume opportunity for new franchise partners entering the market now.

How the Franchise Model Works

Step 1 — Get Set Up

Once your application is approved, you receive full onboarding covering operational training, business setup guidance, equipment and service standards, and access to the booking platform. Most new partners are operational within days.

Step 2 — Start Receiving Jobs

After onboarding, confirmed service requests begin arriving based on your chosen territory. Customers have already received pricing upfront and paid a deposit — jobs are ready to execute when they reach you.

Step 3 — Complete Work and Get Paid

You complete tyre fitting jobs and earn per job completed. The system is designed for simplicity and scalability — no admin, no chasing invoices, no marketing. You focus entirely on delivering the service.

How Much Can You Earn?

Mobile tyre fitting is a high-demand, job-based business where income is directly linked to completed work. There is no salary cap — your earnings scale with the jobs you complete.

Revenue Per Job

Each completed job typically generates from £170 per job, depending on service type, location, and customer requirement. Standard tyre replacements, run-flat fitting, and emergency callouts each carry different rates.

Daily Job Volume

Most active operators complete 6 to 10 jobs per day. Actual volume depends on working hours, territory demand, and seasonal fluctuations. Urban and high-density postcodes consistently generate the highest volumes.

Example Earnings Scenario

A typical active operator working full days:

  • 6 jobs/day × £170 = £1,020/day
  • 10 jobs/day × £170 = £1,700/day

Earnings vary depending on demand, availability, and operational capacity. Figures shown are illustrative based on typical job values and volume.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need experience in tyre fitting?

No prior experience is required. Full operational training is provided as part of your onboarding. You will be guided through everything you need to know before taking your first job.

How quickly can I start?

Most operators are active and receiving jobs within days of approval. Applications are reviewed within 24 hours and onboarding is completed promptly after payment is confirmed.

Is this a full-time or part-time opportunity?

Both are possible. You control your working hours and territory coverage. Many operators start part-time and transition to full-time as job volume and income grow.

How is income generated?

Income is earned per completed job. Each job generates revenue from the service completed, with typical values starting from £170 per job depending on the service type and location.

Is territory exclusive?

Yes. Postcode areas are assigned on a first-come basis. Once you hold a territory, other franchise partners cannot operate in that postcode. Check availability in your area by submitting your application.

How does the franchise fee work?

You pay a monthly fee per postcode territory, starting from £25/month. The fee covers brand access, platform access, job routing, and operational support. There is no large upfront franchise payment.

How many territories can I hold?

There is no upper limit. You can hold as many postcode territories as your operational capacity allows, and expand at any time during your franchise term.

What happens if I want to stop?

The minimum franchise term is 12 months. After that, you can choose to continue, expand, or wind down your territories with appropriate notice.
