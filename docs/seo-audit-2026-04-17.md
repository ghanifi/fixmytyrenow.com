## Critical Issues

### C-1 — FAQPage Schema Contradicts Visible Content on All 160 Combo Pages

**Affects:** All 160 `areas/{borough}/{service}/index.html` pages
**Risk:** Google manual action for structured data mismatch

The `FAQPage` JSON-LD in the `<head>` of every combo page contains old stub answers from before the content rewrite. The schema was never updated when the body content was regenerated.

**Examples found on `areas/bromley/emergency-tyre-replacement/index.html`:**

| | Schema says | Page body says |
|---|---|---|
| Payment methods | "credit/debit cards and cash" | "Cash is not accepted" |
| Arrival time | "30 to 60 minutes" | "20 minutes" |

Google's structured data guidelines explicitly flag contradictions between schema and visible content as a quality violation. Across 160 pages this is a systemic risk to the entire domain.

**Fix:** Update `scripts/rewrite_combo_pages.py` to generate FAQPage schema from the actual HTML FAQ content, or remove FAQPage schema from combo pages entirely.

---

### C-2 — Price Inconsistency: £79 vs £69 for Emergency Tyre Replacement

**File:** `index.html` (homepage service card)

```html
<!-- WRONG — homepage service card -->
<div class="svc-price">From £79 inc. fitting</div>

<!-- CORRECT — everywhere else -->
services/emergency-tyre-replacement/index.html: "From £69"
booking form: "Emergency Tyre Replacement — from £69"
llms.txt: "Emergency Tyre Replacement — from £69"
```

**Fix:** Change `£79` to `£69` in the homepage service card (one line).

---

### C-3 — H1 and First H2 Identical on ~128 of 160 Combo Pages

Every combo page has `<h1>Emergency Tyre Replacement in Bromley</h1>` and immediately inside `borough-intro`, `<h2>Emergency Tyre Replacement in Bromley</h2>` — identical text.

This wastes a heading level, signals templated production, and is a quality marker Google's systems detect.

**Fix:** In `scripts/rewrite_combo_pages.py`, change:
```python
f'<h2>{svc["name"]} in {b["name"]}</h2>\n'
```
to:
```python
f'<h2>Our {svc["name"]} Service in {b["name"]}</h2>\n'
```
Then re-run the script across all 160 pages.

## High Priority Issues

### H-1 — No Service Schema on Combo Pages

**Affects:** All 160 combo pages

Combo pages carry `AutomotiveBusiness`, `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList`, and `Review` schema — but **no `Service` entity**. These are the highest commercial-intent pages on the site. A `Service` block with `price`, `areaServed`, and `aggregateRating` makes them eligible for Service rich results.

**Recommended addition per combo page:**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Service",
  "name": "Emergency Tyre Replacement in Bromley",
  "description": "24/7 emergency mobile tyre replacement in Bromley. Average arrival 20 minutes.",
  "url": "https://fixmytyrenow.com/areas/bromley/emergency-tyre-replacement/",
  "provider": {
    "@type": "AutomotiveBusiness",
    "@id": "https://fixmytyrenow.com/#business",
    "name": "FixMyTyreNow",
    "url": "https://fixmytyrenow.com",
    "telephone": "+447340645595"
  },
  "areaServed": {
    "@type": "AdministrativeArea",
    "name": "Bromley, London"
  },
  "offers": {
    "@type": "Offer",
    "price": 69,
    "priceCurrency": "GBP",
    "availability": "https://schema.org/InStock"
  },
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.9",
    "reviewCount": 912,
    "bestRating": "5",
    "worstRating": "1"
  }
}
```

### H-2 — No Business Schema on About Page

**File:** `about/index.html`

The About page has only a `BreadcrumbList`. No `AutomotiveBusiness` or `Organization` entity. The About page is the primary page Google uses for entity verification.

**Fix:** Add `AutomotiveBusiness` block with `@id: "#business"`, full address, telephone, email, `foundingDate`, `sameAs` (Companies House URL).

---

### H-3 — Reviews Page Has Zero Review Content

**File:** `reviews/index.html`

The page exists, is indexed, and has schema — but 0 review cards are rendered. A business with 912 reviews at 4.9 stars sending users to an empty page is a critical trust failure.

**Fix:** Add at least 20-30 actual customer reviews with: star rating, reviewer name, date, location (e.g. "John T., Orpington"), review text.

---

### H-4 — About Page: No Named Individual (E-E-A-T Gap)

**File:** `about/index.html`

The About page (1,242 words) contains zero named individuals. E-E-A-T score: **50/100**. The page shows multiple AI-generated content signals:

- H1 "About FixMyTyreNow" and first H2 "About FixMyTyreNow" are identical
- Every section follows a statement + bullet list pattern
- Generic phrasing throughout: "We are not just another tyre company"
- No specific dates, events, or unique business narrative
- No technician credentials, certifications, or trade body affiliations

**Fix:** Add a founder section with name, background, and motivation. Add at least one named technician with credentials (REACT, NVQ Level 2 Automotive, etc.).

---

### H-5 — NAP: Address Missing from About Page

`about/index.html` has 0 address mentions. Homepage and contact page both include the address. NAP consistency across all pages is a local SEO ranking factor.

**Fix:** Add "86-90 Paul Street, London, EC2A 4NE" to the About page body.

### M-4 — Postcode Inconsistency on Area Pages

**Example:** `areas/bromley/index.html`

- Meta description: "We cover BR1-BR7, SE20"
- Postcode chips on page: BR1-BR8, SE9 (SE20 absent)

SE20 (Penge/Anerley) is part of the London Borough of Bromley. A user from SE20 would not see their postcode listed after clicking through. Audit all 32 area pages for this type of mismatch.

---

### M-5 — "Coming Soon" Blog Placeholders Are a Trust Negative

**File:** `index.html` (blog section)

Three blog articles are shown as "Coming soon". Placeholder content visible to users undermines trust claims.

**Fix:** Remove the blog section from the homepage until articles are published, or publish the three planned articles:
- "When Your Tyres Need Replacing"
- "What to Do When You Get a Flat Tyre in London"
- "Budget vs. Premium Tyres"

All three are high-value SEO topics that would also improve AI citation readiness.

---

### M-6 — Static Relative Review Dates Will Go Stale

Homepage shows "2 weeks ago", "1 month ago" as static HTML. These become meaningless over time.

**Fix:** Replace with hardcoded real dates (e.g. "March 2026") or connect to a live review feed.
